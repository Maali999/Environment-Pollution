How environment is pollution ?

Environmental pollution is one of the greatest problems the world is facing. It causes extreme harm to human health and affects the mortality rate. The burden of pollution is shared by developed and developing nations alike. Environmental pollution is caused due to various reasons like industrialisation. Forest wildfires, demolition of buildings, improper waste management, and various other reasons contribute to it. There are strict laws in place to prevent pollution in some countries, but still, the long-term effects of the phenomenon can be seen and felt. Environmental pollution leads to serious health issues and affects the sustainable living of the people. Waste that is accumulated is the most visible form of pollution. Every year, billions of tonnes of waste is produced. The issue of waste management is a serious one. The after-effects of pollution are severe and can lead to major loss of life for both humans and animals.

Human activities are responsible for increasing pollution day by day around the world. This has become a great concern, which needs to be addressed immediately. From transportation to agriculture, human activities are polluting the environment. Smoke emitted from vehicles pollutes the air, while pesticides and fertilisers used for agriculture pollute our land. Excessive noise from loudspeakers, traffic, factories, etc., causes noise pollution. Due to this, respiratory diseases have become common. Moreover, it needs to be understood that the effects of pollution can be witnessed for years.
